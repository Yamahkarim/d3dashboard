Smoking vs. Income: An analysis

Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

An analysis of the household median income vs. smoking rates shows a clear negative correlation.This reveals that as median incomes increase, the rates of smoking will decrease.

This finding unravels more questions for causation.
According to the Center for Disease Control (CDC), education may be one major factor.

"In the U.S., people living below the poverty level and people having lower levels of educational attainment have higher rates of cigarette smoking than the general population."
